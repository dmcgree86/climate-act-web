Mandatory Climate‑Related Financial Reporting — Australia

Climate‑related financial reporting is changing. This factsheet summarises the legal basis, core disclosure requirements and the phased timeline for compliance with Australia’s new regime. Under AASB 2, physical climate risks are explicitly required to be disclosed, making robust, defendable quantification and financial interpretation critical.

Legal Basis

Australia’s mandatory climate‑related financial disclosure regime is established through the Treasury Laws Amendment (Financial Market Infrastructure and Other Measures) Act 2024, which amends the Corporations Act 2001. Entities above certain thresholds must prepare a Sustainability Report as part of their annual reporting, in accordance with the Australian Sustainability Reporting Standards (ASRS) issued by the AASB. The primary standard for climate‑related disclosures is AASB 2, based on IFRS S2 with modifications for Australia.

Core Disclosure Requirement

Entities must disclose material information on climate‑related risks and opportunities that could reasonably be expected to affect their prospects, including impacts on:

  • Strategy
  • Business model
  • Cash flows
  • Access to finance
  • Cost of capital

Risk disclosures must cover both physical risks — acute and chronic hazards such as cyclones, floods, droughts, bushfires, heatwaves and sea‑level rise — and transition risks, if material. Entities should specify time horizons (short, medium and long term) over which the effects of each risk or opportunity could reasonably be expected to occur.

Phase‑In Timeline

Group Start Date Thresholds & Inclusions Scope
1 1 Jan 2025 Revenue ≥ AU$500 m; Assets ≥ AU$1 b; Employees ≥ 500; NGER reporters ≥ 50 kt CO₂‑e Full disclosure of material climate‑related risks and opportunities
2 1 Jul 2026 Revenue ≥ AU$200 m; Assets ≥ AU$500 m; Employees ≥ 250; Other NGER reporters; Asset owners with AUM ≥ AU$5 b Same as above
3 1 Jul 2027 Revenue ≥ AU$50 m; Assets ≥ AU$25 m; Employees ≥ 100 Same standard; entities may submit a “no material climate risks” statement with justification

Why Physical Risk Matters

AASB 2 explicitly requires reporting on climate‑related physical risks. To meet these obligations and support strategic planning, organisations need defensible quantification of hazard impacts, translation into financial implications and clear communication of materiality.
